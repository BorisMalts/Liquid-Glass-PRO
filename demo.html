Caustic engine · v4.0.0

Improved Voronoi

PCG2D hash, F2−F1 distance field, domain warping, per-cell depth variation and 11.25° rotation stagger across six octaves.

v3 — Old
sin()-hash · F1 · 4 octaves
Trigonometric hash with visible directional lattice at low frequencies. F1 metric creates pillow artefacts at cell centres. Fixed grid, shared animation template for all cells.
v4 — New
PCG2D · F2−F1 · 6 octaves
Integer PCG2D — no lattice bias, period 2³² per axis. F2−F1 peaks sharply at cell boundaries for tight caustic filaments. Domain warp + per-cell depth + 11.25° rotation stagger per octave.
🎲
PCG2D Integer Hash
Jarzynski & Olano (2020) — two interleaved PCG streams with XOR-shift mixing. No sin/cos. Period 2³² per axis. Zero lattice bias or directional correlation.
📐
F2−F1 Distance Field
Second-nearest minus nearest Voronoi distance peaks exactly at cell boundaries — producing tight caustic filaments that match real underwater light patterns.
🌊
Domain Warping
Low-frequency gnoise field pre-distorts UV before Voronoi evaluation. Breaks global square lattice regularity — cells look truly organic at all scales.
🔄
Rotation Stagger
Each of six octaves rotates by 11.25° (π/16) from the previous. Prevents star-burst alignment artefacts from parallel Voronoi grids at different scales.
